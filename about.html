About me

Born to build

From a very young age, I've been very creative. Don't get me wrong, I recognize that I am still quite young, and that it is a blessing. When I was six, my mother started teaching me Origami. When I was ten, my brother and I received our first PC, and I taught myself how to make "animations" and games in Microsoft PowerPoint. I also discovered MovieMaker and DVDMaker. Later, I joined a youth swing-choir called Younger Generation. When I was twelve I mowed my yard all summer and earned myself a Mac Mini and eventually my own monitor. The next year I received a Pebble smartwatch for Christmas and my interest in Computer programming began. I went to a summer camp in Austin, TX, the following summer and was a T.A. the next year. Now, I am involved in my high school's swing and a capella choirs, drama club, fine arts academic team, and YearBook committee. I am a big fan of animation, especially from Pixar or Makoto Shinkai. I love geeking out about user interaction and reaction. For my future, I am looking at Purdue or even studying in Germany.
